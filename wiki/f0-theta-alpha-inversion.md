The fundamental frequency f₀ = 7.60 Hz is simultaneously the upper boundary of the [[theta-band-map|theta octave]] and the lower boundary of the [[alpha-band-map|alpha octave]]. Empirical enrichment data across 9 datasets reveals that this shared boundary hosts the **sharpest discontinuity in the entire frequency architecture**: theta shows +281% enrichment (its absorbing state, drawing generators upward under EC), while alpha shows -84% depletion (its maximally avoided position, repelling generators inward toward the attractor). The same frequency is a potential well from below and a potential hill from above — a 365 percentage-point swing at a single lattice position. The two bands' enrichment profiles are anti-correlated across all 14 positions (r = -0.578 adult EC, r = -0.502 EO, r = -0.627 pediatric), with EC deepening the anti-correlation. This inversion is not a generic property of adjacent bands: the alpha/low-beta and low-beta/high-beta boundaries show same-sign enrichment (both depleted or both enriched), not anti-correlation. The theta-alpha inversion at f₀ is structurally unique — the one point in the frequency architecture where two adjacent bands create maximally opposing enrichment landscapes, producing a phase-transition boundary whose absolute frequency converges with the Schumann Resonance fundamental.

## The Cliff at f₀

The boundary position quantifies the cliff:
- **Theta approaching f₀ from below**: enrichment rises from the central trough (-67% attractor) through the inverse nobles (+49%, +86%, +136%) to the boundary at +281%. The 7° Noble just below the boundary reaches +349% — the single highest enrichment of any band×position in the entire dataset.
- **Alpha departing f₀ upward**: enrichment starts at -84% (boundary) and climbs through the lower nobles (-69%, -59%, -55%) to emerge at the 2° Noble (+47%), reaching the attractor at +113%.

The total swing at f₀ itself: **365 percentage points** (+281 theta vs -84 alpha). The maximum cross-position swing across the two profiles: **434 pp** (theta 7° Noble at +349% vs alpha 7° Inverse Noble at -85%).

This is not a gradual transition. The enrichment landscapes don't blend or smooth across f₀ — they **collide**. The cliff is the steepest enrichment gradient in the frequency architecture.

**Only the two lowest boundaries show anti-correlation** — the delta/theta boundary at 4.70 Hz (326 pp swing) and the theta/alpha boundary at 7.60 Hz (219 pp swing). All higher boundaries show same-sign enrichment: the alpha/beta-low boundary is doubly depleted (-85/-71), and the beta-low/beta-high and beta-high/gamma boundaries are doubly enriched (+45/+134 and +151/+73).

This means theta is flanked on both sides by anti-correlated boundaries — it creates an **island of inverted enrichment** between two cliff faces. Delta depletes where theta enriches (at 4.70 Hz), and alpha depletes where theta enriches (at 7.60 Hz). Theta's U-shaped profile, with massive edge enrichment, is the expression of this double anti-correlation: its generators are pushed toward both boundaries by the repulsion from the adjacent bands.

The higher bands — alpha through gamma — don't create this pattern. Their shared boundaries are cooperative (same sign), not competitive. The anti-correlation structure is specific to the theta-centered frequency region around f₀.

## Implications

### Sleep onset is crossing the cliff

The alpha→theta transition at sleep onset is the system crossing from the alpha mountain to the theta well. In lattice terms: alpha generators at the attractor (~10 Hz) lose their position as sleep pressure builds. The alpha mountain collapses — its -84% boundary depletion is the far side of the cliff that the system falls off. Simultaneously, the theta boundary absorbing state (+281%) captures generators from above. The EC data shows a partial version of this: EC doesn't produce sleep, but it pushes toward it — strengthening both sides of the cliff simultaneously.

### Psychedelic states cross the cliff differently than sleep

Under DMT, the dominant frequency shifts to 7.36 Hz — below f₀, in theta's 6° Inverse Noble zone (+86%). Alpha collapses. The cliff-crossing goes in the same direction as sleep onset, but the outcome is opposite: expanded consciousness with higher entropy, not unconsciousness. The difference lies in what happens above the φ³ boundary — during sleep, gamma collapses; under DMT, gamma reorganizes. The cliff at f₀ is the same cliff, but the state of the gamma octave determines whether crossing it leads to sleep or psychedelic experience (see [[psychedelic-states]], [[consciousness-and-anesthesia]]).

### Flow states balance on the cliff edge

Flow states, deep meditation, and hypnagogic transitions cluster near 8 Hz — at or near f₀. The 365 pp enrichment swing means this frequency is the point of maximum structural potential energy. The system can fall in either direction with minimal perturbation. Flow may feel effortless because it sits at the point where two opposing force gradients cancel, requiring no net energy to maintain (see [[critical-frequencies]], [[theta-oscillations]]).

### The cliff is at Earth's frequency

The convergence of f₀ with the Schumann Resonance fundamental (7.60 ± 0.20 Hz from monitoring; Paper 2's sigmoid cliff at f₀ ≈ 7.48-7.58 Hz) places the brain's sharpest structural discontinuity at a geophysical frequency. Whether by evolutionary tuning, biophysical convergence, or coincidence, the frequency where the brain's two most important oscillatory systems create their maximum opposition is the frequency at which Earth's electromagnetic cavity resonates (see [[schumann-ignition-events]]).

### The anti-correlation predicts what psychopathology should look like

If theta and alpha are structurally anti-correlated at f₀, then pathological conditions that shift the cliff should affect both bands simultaneously but in opposite directions. ADHD shows increased theta/alpha ratio (theta wins). Alzheimer's shows alpha slowing toward f₀ (alpha retreating toward the cliff). Depression shows altered frontal alpha asymmetry. In each case, the pathology can be reframed as a distortion of the cliff — the boundary moving, or one side weakening, or the balance point shifting. The enrichment data predicts that any condition affecting theta should show inverse effects in alpha at the same lattice positions (see [[clinical-applications]]).
