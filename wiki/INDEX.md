## Unified Framework
- [[unified-phi-lattice-framework]] - Complete synthesis of all φ-lattice findings: ten empirical pillars (equation, three-paper validation, six band-specific enrichment fingerprints, f₀ theta-alpha inversion, coupling geometry, molecular oscillator alignment, developmental shifts, two-grid architecture, clock-to-lattice developmental transition, testable predictions), three nested organizational levels (molecular clocks → lattice positions → coupling network), what the framework explains and what it doesn't, and the "structural fossil" characterization — real, narrow, structurally specific, and cognitively inert. The lattice is a developmental endpoint that children's brains grow into as GABA-A subunits mature (α2→α1, ~age 22), after which it becomes species-level constant, cognitively inert, and structurally invariant across adult aging.
- [[phi-lattice-predictions]] - 25 testable predictions derived from the framework: Tier 1 (existing data — band shape replication, f₀ cliff pathology, PAC lattice tracking, developmental gamma gradient), Tier 2 (new experiments — pharmacological position shifts, cross-species f₀ scaling, intracranial validation, IRASA replication), Tier 3 (long-range — emergent lattice in artificial systems, lattice disruption impairs cognition). Two critical-priority predictions (IRASA method-independence, gamma 80+ Hz extension) require only re-analysis of existing data.

## Frequency Bands
- [[canonical-eeg-bands]] - Comprehensive reference on the five canonical EEG frequency bands (delta, theta, alpha, beta, gamma), their phi-n geometric organization, and the empirical evidence for golden-ratio scaling of neural oscillation frequencies. Covers the historical evolution of band nomenclature from Berger through modern spectral parameterization, the inconsistency problem in band definitions across studies, sub-band functional distinctions within each canonical band, individualized band definitions anchored to IAF, harmonic confounds from non-sinusoidal waveforms, extended frequency ranges (infraslow and high-frequency oscillations), the rhythmicity dimension (sustained vs. bursty bands), and cross-frequency coupling hierarchies.
- [[delta-oscillations]] - Covers delta oscillations (0.5-4 Hz) as the brain's primary mechanism for homeostatic restoration, synaptic downscaling, and large-scale state regulation during sleep. Details the cortical slow oscillation's Up/Down state dynamics including Layer 5 initiation, astrocytic timing, and HCN channel modulation, alongside the causal demonstration that synaptic potentiation drives NREM delta power (Sawada et al., 2024). Addresses delta's role as master phase organizer during N3 sleep, the SWR-spindle-slow oscillation nesting hierarchy for memory consolidation, entorhinal cortex delta generators, delta in anesthesia and torpor, the "consciousness among delta waves" paradox, nucleus reuniens pathological delta in schizophrenia, age-related SWA decline linked to neurodegeneration, neonatal delta brushes as cortical maturation markers, and waking delta in cognitive control and reward processing.
- [[delta-band-map]] - The delta region spans five φ-octaves (n=-6 to -1, 0.42-4.70 Hz) but only the uppermost is spectrally resolvable. Weak enrichment (+35% attractor, +42% 4° Noble anomaly) at 3-10× lower magnitudes than other bands. Maps all five octaves to functional content (infraslow neuromodulation, slow oscillation up/down states, SWA homeostasis, core delta, fast delta/delta-theta transition) with frank assessment of multi-octave pooling, resolution floor, and aperiodic confound limitations.
- [[theta-oscillations]] - Covers theta oscillations (4-8 Hz) as the brain's primary temporal scaffold for memory encoding, spatial navigation, cognitive control, and sequential planning. Details the 8 Hz nexus at the theta-alpha boundary, cholinergic control of theta during encoding, theta-gamma phase-amplitude coupling in navigation with dual gamma sub-band dissociation, frontal midline theta in cognitive control and decision-making, theta-gamma PAC as a ubiquitous cognitive marker, theta phase precession beyond place cells (including head direction cells), theta's roles in sleep memory consolidation via thalamic theta bursts and REM theta-ripple chains, theta burst stimulation for clinical therapeutics, and FM-theta neurofeedback for cognitive enhancement. Also covers theta signatures in meditation, psychedelic states, and mind wandering.
- [[theta-band-map]] - Comprehensive φ-lattice map of the theta band (4.70-7.60 Hz): all 15 positions through degree 7, three functional zones (lower PE processing, central theta core, upper convergence corridor), specific frequencies mapped to functions, EC theta convergence dynamics, and the f₀ absorbing state.
- [[alpha-oscillations]] - Covers alpha oscillations (8-13 Hz) as the dominant rhythm of the resting human brain, implementing active inhibitory gating of perception, information routing, and consciousness. Details the thalamocortical origin and Berger effect, competing models of alpha rhythmogenesis, the inhibition-timing hypothesis refined by indirect load-dependent mechanisms, three discrete alpha archetypes with distinct cortical sources, alpha traveling waves carrying bidirectional predictive coding signals, alpha-gamma cross-frequency coupling, frontal alpha asymmetry as an affective biomarker, IAF's role in temporal perception and body ownership, alpha changes in aging and neurodegeneration, and alpha-based neurofeedback and stimulation protocols for PTSD, cognitive aging, and meditation.
- [[alpha-band-map]] - Comprehensive φ-lattice map of the alpha band (7.60-12.30 Hz): all 15 positions through degree 7, three functional zones (lower transition, central alpha core at the 1° Noble, upper perceptual timing edge), IAF-lattice convergence, consciousness gating, EC amplification dynamics, the alpha-IAF confound, and integration across all wiki topics.
- [[beta-oscillations]] - Covers beta oscillations (13-30 Hz) as a functionally heterogeneous band comprising at least three subtypes with distinct laminar origins: beta1 from superficial layers via period concatenation, beta2 from deep Layer V via gap junctions and KCNQ channels, and beta3 at the gamma transition. Details the 20 Hz motor convergence point and status quo hypothesis, corticomuscular beta burst propagation, beta's role in working memory priority switching and distractor suppression, prediction error encoding in the beta range, frequency-multiplexed categorical decision signals, beta burst dynamics at criticality, clinical significance in Parkinson's disease including FDA-approved adaptive DBS, and the finding that cortical beta maps onto shared dopamine-modulated cortico-subcortical networks.
- [[low-beta-band-map]] - Comprehensive φ-lattice map of the low beta band (12.30-19.90 Hz): all 15 positions through degree 7, three functional zones (spindle/alpha-beta transition, predictive coding core at Noble1 ~16.5 Hz, motor PMBR zone at inverse nobles ~19 Hz), paradigm-dependent position profiles, dual generator systems (superficial beta1 vs deep beta2), and the exceptional φ² boundary at ~20 Hz.
- [[high-beta-band-map]] - Comprehensive φ-lattice map of the high beta band (19.90-32.19 Hz): bimodal edge-loading profile (lower ~20 Hz motor peak +134%, upper ~31 Hz transition peak +151%, depleted center ~25-27 Hz), dramatic EC amplification (7.4× at boundary), 25 Hz premotor attractor, Parkinson's pathological zone, the φ³ consciousness gate at 32 Hz, and extreme pediatric 4° Inv Noble +346% developmental biomarker at ~30 Hz.
- [[gamma-oscillations]] - Covers gamma oscillations (30-100+ Hz) generated by PV+ interneuron-pyramidal cell PING/ING mechanisms in superficial cortical layers. Details the 40 Hz phenomenon, CP-AMPAR self-reinforcing plasticity of gamma, gamma's relationship to consciousness (local vs. long-range synchronization, ketamine dissociation, dying brain gamma surges), theta-gamma coupling in working memory, the GENUS 40 Hz entrainment approach for Alzheimer's disease (including 2-year open-label data and pivotal HOPE trial pending), the GAMER hypothesis of endogenous gamma as circuit health guardian, gamma deficits in schizophrenia as translational biomarker, high gamma/broadband HFA as distinct from narrowband gamma, and critical methodological caveats about muscle artifact contamination and aperiodic confounds in scalp EEG gamma measurements.
- [[gamma-band-map]] - Comprehensive φ-lattice map of the gamma band (32.19-52.09 Hz): attractor/Noble1 enrichment (+79/+102% adult EC), 45 Hz FOOOF ceiling truncating the upper octave, extreme dataset divergence (LEMON boundary +319% vs CHBMP Noble1 -60%), pediatric 2° Noble shift (+181% at ~39 Hz reflecting immature GABA-A), condition-invariance (gamma barely changes EC vs EO), and the unmeasured upper-octave territory above 45 Hz.

## Mechanisms
- [[thalamocortical-circuits]] - Covers the reciprocal thalamocortical loops that generate and gate the brain's dominant oscillatory rhythms, with the thalamic reticular nucleus (TRN) as an inhibitory gating hub using T-type calcium channel dynamics. Details alpha generation via the thalamocortical loop, TRN developmental refinement via LRRTM3 and dual inhibitory architecture, sleep spindle generation (fast vs. slow) and their role in coordinating co-ripples for memory consolidation, core vs. matrix thalamocortical projection systems, higher-order thalamic nuclei (pulvinar, mediodorsal) in attention and cognition, thalamocortical contributions to consciousness, thalamocortical dysrhythmia as a transdiagnostic pathological state, and Layer 6 corticothalamic feedback modulation revealed by optogenetic and chemogenetic tools.
- [[cross-frequency-coupling]] - Covers the mechanisms by which neural oscillations at different frequencies interact to coordinate brain activity across temporal and spatial scales, focusing on phase-amplitude coupling (PAC) and phase-phase coupling. Details theta-gamma PAC as both a working memory buffer and navigation code, alpha-gamma coupling as a sensory gating mechanism, cell-type contributions (PV for gamma, SST for theta), CFC shifts during consciousness transitions and sleep-dependent memory consolidation, expanding clinical applications in Parkinson's disease (beta-gamma PAC as DBS biomarker), epilepsy (HFO-based seizure detection), and psychiatric disorders. Includes critical methodological discussion of spurious PAC from non-sinusoidal waveform harmonics and approaches for confound control.
- [[lattice-coupling-geometry]] - Position-to-position coupling analysis: φⁿ coupling preserves lattice position across octaves (attractor→attractor), 2:1 harmonic shifts by +0.440 creating deterministic position pairs (Boundary→Attractor, Noble1→6°Noble), the enrichment-coupling paradox (PAC connects depleted positions, not enrichment peaks), Fibonacci three-wave resonance at boundaries, and predictions for task-evoked PAC frequency shifts.
- [[neural-resonance-and-entrainment]] - Covers intrinsic neural resonance frequencies, sensory entrainment effects on cognition, and inter-brain synchronization during social interaction. Documents the Arnold tongue framework for predicting individual entrainment responses, the entrainment-versus-evoked-response debate, and spectral tuning/after-effects as evidence for active oscillatory adjustment. Includes comprehensive coverage of tACS for cognitive enhancement, 40 Hz gamma entrainment for neurodegeneration (with 2-year Alzheimer's follow-up showing pTau217 reductions), binaural beats, and state-dependent modulation of entrainment success.
- [[brain-computation]] - Synthesizes the case that neural oscillations serve as active computational mechanisms rather than epiphenomena, covering spatial computing via alpha/beta inhibitory stencils, ephaptic coupling with field-to-neuron interactions an order of magnitude stronger than the reverse, traveling waves that create temporary synaptic networks via STDP, and communication through coherence. Addresses the ~10 bits/s information bottleneck of conscious processing, theta-band phase coding optimization, the brain's extraordinary energy efficiency (~20 W, with cognition adding only ~5%), and oscillation-inspired AI architectures including LinOSS, DONN, HoloBrain, and Rhythm-SNN.
- [[cortical-layer-oscillations]] - Details the spectrolaminar motif -- superficial-layer gamma and deep-layer alpha/beta -- validated across 14 cortical areas in primates, with the crossover frequency near 32 Hz aligning with the Layer 4 boundary. Covers interneuron mechanisms (PING vs. ING, PV+ perisomatic vs. SST+ dendritic targeting), cellular basis linking PV/CB/CR interneuron density to oscillation power, laminar predictive coding where gamma carries feedforward prediction errors and alpha/beta carries feedback predictions, Layer 6 corticothalamic control of oscillations, high-density Neuropixels recording advances, and laminar fMRI dissociations of imagery versus perception.
- [[aperiodic-dynamics]] - Explains the broadband 1/f power-law component of neural spectra and why separating it from periodic oscillations is methodologically essential -- demonstrated by the Masina warning, where an apparent alpha power decrease was entirely an aperiodic offset shift. Covers the aperiodic exponent as an E/I balance proxy (validated by GABAergic pharmacology but complicated by NMDA insensitivity and frequency-range dependence), its lifespan trajectory mirroring biological aging markers, and state-dependent changes under psychedelics, anesthesia, and sleep. Reviews clinical applications across ADHD, schizophrenia, depression, Alzheimer's, and epilepsy, and notes that ~77% of putative awake hippocampal ripples may be 1/f false positives.
- [[critical-frequencies]] - Key frequency nexus points (8, 12, 20, 25, 32, 40 Hz) where biophysical constraints, cognitive functions, and clinical phenomena converge, irrational advantage of φ-spacing over rationals for cross-frequency coupling, two-level organization (octave + sub-octave), exponential scaling validation, and per-position signal structure in all-band aggregate (noble2 null, degree-3 repellers — but per-band profiles diverge: e.g., alpha noble2 = +47% EC, low-beta inverse nobles massively enriched).
- [[schumann-ignition-events]] - Transient episodes of multi-band network synchronization at phi-n frequencies; discovery context, independence-convergence paradox, substrate-ignition model, anchor convergence across studies, sigmoid cliff at Schumann eigenfrequency, EC theta convergence on f0 (Dortmund sign-flip), and relationship to Schumann Resonance (Lacy, 2026; 2026b; 2026c).
- [[phi-lattice-enrichment-data]] - Comprehensive empirical enrichment data across 9 datasets (4 adult, 5 pediatric), 14 positions, 6 bands: band-specific position fingerprints (theta→boundary, alpha→attractor/Noble1, gamma→Noble1, low-beta→inverse nobles, high-beta→bimodal boundary), adult vs pediatric divergence (gamma Noble1→Noble2 shift), summary statistics, and methodological notes on OT vs standard extraction.
- [[f0-theta-alpha-inversion]] - The sharpest discontinuity in the frequency architecture: theta (+281%) and alpha (-84%) create opposing enrichment landscapes at f₀ = 7.60 Hz, a 365 pp cliff. Covers what scales (hippocampal theta inversely with brain size) versus what does not (cortical alpha, beta, gamma frequencies preserved), the spectrolaminar motif's cross-species validation, convergent oscillatory solutions in insects including Drosophila beta-range binding and honeybee alpha-like 18 Hz rhythms, reptilian sharp-wave ripples, sleep-dependent infraslow rhythms conserved across amniotes for ~300 million years, zebrafish sleep states, pre-nervous system oscillations in cnidarians, and the universal rhythmic architecture of sustained vs. bursty bands.
- [[neurochemistry-of-oscillations]] - Traces the causal chain from molecular time constants to macroscopic EEG oscillations, showing how GABA-A IPSC decay (~25 ms) sets the 40 Hz gamma clock, M-current and h-current generate beta and theta resonance, and T-type calcium channels drive thalamic alpha and spindle pacemaking. ## Functions
- [[motor-control]] - Covers the oscillatory dynamics of motor planning, execution, and learning, centering on beta desynchronization-rebound cycles that are non-focal, propagate across somatotopic regions, and are actively inhibitory via GABA-A mechanisms. Details functionally distinct roles for alpha (reaction time) and beta (accuracy) corticomuscular coherence, movement-related gamma synchronization with low-gamma/high-gamma phase-amplitude coupling, and cerebellar contributions including precise frequency encoding and predictive beta signaling. Addresses tremor pathophysiology in Parkinson's disease versus essential tremor, motor learning biomarkers, and stimulation interventions including beta-frequency DBS and theta cerebellar tACS.
- [[working-memory]] - Synthesizes the oscillatory architecture of working memory, centering on the beta-gamma push-pull model where deep-layer beta gates superficial-layer gamma bursts that refresh synaptic traces, replacing the classic persistent-firing account. Covers alpha phase synchronization networks, beta sub-bands managing priority through representational rotation, theta-gamma phase-amplitude coupling scaling with load and predicting capacity via hippocampal PAC neurons (Daume et al., 2024), activity-silent working memory through latent synaptic traces, age-related oscillatory decline reversible by targeted tACS, and clinical biomarkers in schizophrenia and ADHD.
- [[hippocampal-memory]] - Comprehensive account of the oscillatory mechanisms underlying episodic memory encoding, consolidation, and retrieval in the hippocampus. Theta phase precession provides ~10:1 temporal compression for STDP-driven learning, now demonstrated in non-spatial human memory (Zheng et al., 2024), while theta-gamma coupling implements a double dissociation between fast gamma (encoding) and slow gamma (retrieval). Awake sharp-wave ripples selectively tag experiences for sleep consolidation (Yang & Buzsaki, 2024), large sleep ripples causally drive hippocampo-cortical reactivation (Robinson et al., 2025), and replay can occur without ripples (Widloski & Foster, 2025). Also covers the broadband vs. oscillatory theta debate, CA1 sublayer scaffolding, high-frequency oscillatory dialogue, emotional memory gamma reactivation, Alzheimer's disease oscillatory dysfunction, and aperiodic decomposition methodology.
- [[sleep-oscillations]] - Detailed treatment of state-dependent oscillatory architecture across NREM and REM sleep. The slow oscillation-spindle-ripple coupling cascade constitutes the core mechanism of systems memory consolidation, with SOs setting cortical excitability windows, spindles serving as relays, and spindle-locked ripples driving hippocampal memory read-out (Staresina et al., 2023; Schreiner et al., 2024). Covers tonic vs. phasic REM substates, biophysical modeling of TRN spindle generation, respiratory-driven hippocampal oscillations that scaffold coupling (Zelano et al., 2024), norepinephrine-mediated glymphatic clearance during NREM (Jiang et al., 2024), sleep oscillation changes in aging including SO-spindle hierarchy reversal, sleep oscillations as biomarkers of neurodegeneration, closed-loop auditory stimulation, targeted memory reactivation, and REM theta's role in emotional memory processing.
- [[attention-and-perception]] - Examines frequency-specific oscillatory mechanisms governing spatial attention, temporal perception, multisensory integration, and perceptual binding. Alpha phase phasically gates distractor interference while alpha power lateralization does not predict behavior, and the canonical alpha band conceals at least two spatiotemporally distinct oscillator populations. Theta oscillations implement rhythmic attentional sampling that doubles in frequency with conscious awareness, exhibits hexagonal spatial organization linked to grid cells, and alternates working memory item templates at theta rate. Pre-stimulus alpha frequency and phase jointly determine perceptual sensitivity, gamma operates through discrete events with a causal role in visual perception, and gamma synchrony mediates figure-ground segregation through coupled oscillator dynamics.
- [[auditory-and-speech-oscillations]] - Covers the multi-scale oscillatory infrastructure for auditory processing, speech comprehension, music perception, and vocal production. Delta and theta oscillations track syllabic and prosodic structure through both entrainment and evoked response mechanisms, alpha lateralization gates auditory spatial attention and indexes listening effort, and the 40 Hz ASSR serves as a clinical biomarker for schizophrenia and a therapeutic target in Alzheimer's via GENUS stimulation. Extends to sign language cortical tracking in the delta band, tonal language processing, bilingual oscillatory dynamics, singing vs. speaking sensorimotor control, vibrotactile speech enhancement, inter-brain coupling during social interaction, beat perception in premature neonates, and oscillatory reorganization following hearing loss and cochlear implantation.
- [[corticostriatal-learning]] - Details how beta oscillations (~16-25 Hz) serve as the primary communication frequency between prefrontal cortex and striatum during category learning, decision-making, and reward processing. Covers the direct/indirect/hyperdirect pathway anatomy, pathological beta in Parkinson's disease, dopaminergic modulation of reward prediction error through D1/D2 pathways, striatal cholinergic interneurons as beta pacemakers, adaptive deep brain stimulation using beta biomarkers (FDA-approved 2025), habit formation in cortex-independent basal ganglia loops, frequency-specific effort/reward decomposition in decision circuits, and non-invasive striatal stimulation via temporal interference.
- [[predictive-coding]] - Presents the oscillatory implementation of predictive coding through frequency-specific laminar channels: gamma from supragranular layers carries feedforward prediction errors while alpha/beta from infragranular layers carries feedback predictions, with Directed Asymmetry Index correlations exceeding r = 0.9 with anatomical hierarchy. Covers causal microstimulation evidence, beta as prediction carrier, signed prediction error in motor cortex, global prediction error states at theta timescales, the four-frequency prediction hierarchy, Vinck et al.'s (2025) challenge that aperiodic transients rather than sustained oscillations may mediate rapid inference, alpha's dual role as prediction carrier and active inference gate, rhythm-based speech prediction (BRyBI model), and predictive coding disruptions in schizophrenia and autism.
- [[sharp-wave-ripples]] - Comprehensive article on hippocampal sharp-wave ripples (80-200 Hz), covering their generation through CA3 excitation and PV+ interneuron inhibitory rebound, 5-20x temporal compression for STDP, and the two-stage memory selection process where awake SWRs tag experiences (Yang et al., 2024) and large sleep SWRs execute consolidation via hippocampo-cortical reactivation (Robinson et al., 2026). Distinct ripple subtypes (Radsink vs. LMsink) organize parallel reactivation channels. Covers the SO-spindle-ripple consolidation triad with human intracranial evidence, cortical ripples and co-rippling facilitated by thalamic spindles, human SWR functions including emotional memory, semantic retrieval, and spontaneous thought, and clinical relevance in Alzheimer's disease.
- [[consciousness-and-anesthesia]] - Examines the oscillatory boundaries that demarcate consciousness from unconsciousness, particularly the beta-gamma transition around 30-35 Hz. Propofol anteriorization disrupts two distinct thalamocortical networks (Weiner et al., 2023), three molecularly distinct anesthetics convergently destabilize cortical dynamics, while ketamine uniquely preserves gamma and produces dose-dependent stabilization dissociating responsiveness from conscious content. Covers the COGITATE adversarial collaboration (Melloni et al., 2025) challenging both IIT and GNWT, cognitive motor dissociation found in 25% of unresponsive patients (Claassen et al., 2024), lucid dreaming electrophysiology, perturbational complexity index as a consciousness biomarker, and advances in anesthesia depth monitoring beyond BIS.
- [[self-and-embodiment]] - Neural oscillations construct and maintain the sense of self, body ownership, and self-other boundaries through frequency-specific mechanisms: individual alpha frequency gates the temporal binding window for body ownership, pre-movement alpha phase in sensorimotor cortex gates the sense of agency, and alpha-band coupling links the default mode network to self-referential processing. High-beta attenuation at approximately 27 Hz in posterior medial cortex converges across studies as a marker of self-dissolution during meditation. Also covers interoception via heartbeat-evoked potentials, psychedelic ego dissolution through alpha-band criticality shifts, vestibular self-motion perception, and depersonalization-derealization disorder.
- [[default-mode-network]] - Comprehensive account of the DMN's oscillatory physiology, covering its alpha-dominated resting-state signature, intracranial electrophysiology revealing slow-wave intra-network synchronization and beta/gamma cross-network communication, and the mechanistic link between alpha oscillations and DMN connectivity established by alpha-tACS stimulation studies. Traces DMN dynamics across mind wandering, meditation, psychedelic-induced collapse, sleep-dependent memory consolidation, disorders of consciousness, and aging-related network dedifferentiation. Clinical relevance spans Alzheimer's, depression, schizophrenia, and ADHD, with DMN functional connectivity positioned as a transdiagnostic biomarker.
- [[psychedelic-states]] - Alpha power suppression in posterior cortex is the most consistent electrophysiological marker across all classic serotonergic psychedelics, with PCC alpha collapse correlating strongly with ego dissolution. Each substance displays a unique oscillatory fingerprint -- LSD shifts alpha peak frequency upward while restructuring alpha sub-band topography, DMT produces delta/theta emergence resembling REM sleep, 5-MeO-DMT induces a vigilance state dissociation with incoherent complex slow waves, and ketamine drives robust gamma increases via NMDA blockade. Covers criticality shifts under DMT, therapeutic biomarkers including post-psilocybin theta enhancement for depression, microdosing effects on neural complexity, and comparisons to meditation and dreaming states.

## Individual Differences
- [[individual-alpha-frequency]] - IAF varies from 8-12 Hz across individuals, exhibits high heritability (H² = 0.76), and follows a systematic posterior-to-anterior cortical gradient that dynamically forms and dissolves over time. Develops the golden ratio hypothesis linking IAF to canonical band organization through phi-scaling, presents cognitive correlates including temporal perception, body ownership sensitivity, and ADHD traits, and traces IAF's developmental trajectory from a transient 9.5 Hz peak in 2-4 month infants through adult stabilization to age-related slowing. Clinically, IAF informs the Brainmarker-I system for depression treatment stratification, predicts MCI-to-Alzheimer's conversion, and guides personalized brain stimulation protocols.
- [[sex-differences-in-oscillations]] - Biological sex leaves a robust EEG signature decodable at 65-80% accuracy, with females showing faster individual alpha frequency, higher sleep spindle density and sigma power, and cyclic modulation of theta, alpha, and gamma power across the menstrual cycle driven by allopregnanolone's GABAergic modulation. Aging amplifies sex divergence -- low alpha and low beta differences are robust only in older adults -- while developmental sex differences in aperiodic slope emerge after the first year of life. Covers clinical implications including catamenial epilepsy, sex-specific gamma phenotypes in Fragile X syndrome, pregnancy-related coherence changes, and frontal alpha asymmetry as a sex-moderated depression biomarker.

## Development
- [[development-and-aging]] - Traces the brain's oscillatory architecture from delta/theta-dominated neonatal rhythms through childhood alpha emergence, adolescent gamma circuit maturation driven by a SOM+-to-PV+ interneuron switch, adult peak performance near age 28, and age-related aperiodic slope flattening. A landmark 1,563-participant lifespan study identifies monotonically decreasing aperiodic activity and growth-then-decline periodic activity. Covers critical periods marked by transient 40 Hz gamma signatures, premature birth accelerating aperiodic maturation, neurodevelopmental disorders (ADHD, autism) as oscillatory trajectory deviations, and Alzheimer's 40 Hz entrainment therapy showing disease-modifying potential with pTau217 reductions.
- [[developmental-lattice-evolution]] - How the φ-lattice evolves across development: three categories (shape-preserved with r>0.9 for theta/alpha/delta, upward-migrating for gamma/low-beta/high-beta, amplitude-growth for high-beta), the clock-to-lattice transition (φ ranks 7th in all pediatric datasets, 1st-5th in adults — continuous trajectory untested, Prediction P9), adult aging at fixed positions (r=0.006, p=0.877 for ages 20-70). Cross-species maturation scaling (22 years human vs ~5 weeks mouse), the extended human clock-dominated phase as a hypothesized learning window (coupling-biased before lattice crystallization), and Alzheimer's as predicted partial developmental reversal (Prediction P4, untested).
