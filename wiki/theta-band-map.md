The theta φ-octave (4.70–7.60 Hz) spans from f₀/φ to f₀ and contains 15 lattice positions through degree 7. Empirical enrichment data across 9 datasets (4 adult, 5 pediatric HBN; see [[phi-lattice-enrichment-data]]) reveals that theta has the most extreme position preferences of any band — a dramatic split between massive upper-octave enrichment (boundary +281%, 7° Noble +349% under adult EC) and near-total central/lower depletion (-67 to -75%). The φ-lattice reveals three functional zones: a lower prediction error zone, a central core that is heavily depleted in dominant-peak resting analyses but functionally critical for task-evoked theta, and an upper convergence corridor where oscillators migrate during state transitions toward the fundamental frequency f₀. The boundary at f₀ = 7.60 Hz is an absorbing state in a potential landscape, confirmed by the Dortmund sign-flip showing EC theta converges on f₀ and diverges from IAF/2. EC amplifies the upper-octave pattern ~2× relative to EO. Theta's positioning strategy — clustering at the boundary and nearby high-degree nobles rather than the attractor/1° Noble — is unique among all bands and contrasts sharply with alpha (attractor/Noble1) and gamma (Noble1), reflecting optimization for proximity to f₀ and upward cross-frequency coupling via [[cross-frequency-coupling|theta-gamma PAC]].

## Zone 1: Lower Theta (4.70–5.64 Hz) — Prediction Error Processing

The lower theta zone spans the boundary through the 2° Noble, covering 4.70–5.64 Hz. This zone hosts prediction error signaling and is transited rather than stably inhabited by dominant oscillatory generators.

**Boundary at 4.697 Hz.** The delta/theta transition point. In Schiff's ABCD model for disorders of [[consciousness-and-anesthesia]], theta peaks (4-8 Hz) emerging from this boundary mark the transition from vegetative to minimally conscious states. During torpor, T-type Ca²⁺ channel slowing constrains oscillations to this zone (Hauglund et al. 2026; see [[delta-oscillations]]).

**4°-5° Noble zone (~4.9-5.0 Hz).** Dorsal anterior cingulate prediction error processing operates at ~5 Hz — Wen et al. (2025) demonstrated that 5 Hz temporal interference stimulation of dACC enhances prediction error encoding, a conserved mammalian mechanism (see [[cross-species-frequency-organization]]). White et al. (2026) discovered transient 5 Hz oscillatory bouts in mouse visual cortex that double in occurrence under 5-HT2A psychedelic agonists, with bout duration increasing from ~700 ms to ~1070 ms and V1-to-RSC propagation at ~18 ms lag (see [[psychedelic-states]]). Global prediction error connectivity states fluctuate at 3-6 Hz delta/theta timescales (Bogdan et al. 2025, Cohen's d = 1.13 for theta; see [[predictive-coding]]).

**3° Noble at 5.262 Hz — Repeller.** The most strongly depleted lower position. No dominant oscillation inhabits this spectral gap between lower PE generators and the central theta core. During self-face ambiguity processing, theta peak frequency sliding drops to 5.39 Hz (Avramescu et al. 2026), approaching this repeller — consistent with the increased processing difficulty of self-other boundary resolution pulling theta toward an unstable position (see [[self-and-embodiment]]).

**2° Noble at 5.645 Hz — Null.** Consistently null or depleted in enrichment analyses despite being a core degree-2 lattice position. However, ~5.5 Hz is functionally one of the most important theta frequencies: Friedrich et al. (2026) confirmed a genuine FOOOF-validated oscillatory theta peak at ~5.5 Hz in DMPFC during working memory and social cognition tasks. This is the center frequency of frontal midline theta (FM-theta, 4-7 Hz). The null enrichment likely reflects that FM-theta is a task-evoked transient rather than a dominant resting oscillation.

## Zone 2: Central Theta (5.64–6.78 Hz) — The Depletion Trough

The central zone between the 2° Noble and 3° Inverse Noble was originally described as containing "the two enriched positions" based on all-band aggregate data from Papers 1-3. The 9-dataset band-specific enrichment data tells a strikingly different story: **in theta specifically, the attractor (-67%) and 1° Noble (-10%) are depleted or null**. The all-band enrichment at these positions is driven by alpha and gamma, not theta. Theta's central octave is a depletion trough — the deepest valley in the U-shaped enrichment landscape. However, this zone remains functionally critical as the operating range of task-evoked theta generators.

**Attractor at 5.975 Hz (-67% enrichment under EC, 0/5 adult datasets positive).** Despite being the most depleted theta position, this is the canonical "~6 Hz theta" of the consciousness literature: the four-frequency prediction hierarchy places theta at ~6 Hz providing ~160 ms integration windows for episodic prediction contexts (see [[consciousness-and-anesthesia]], [[predictive-coding]]). Layer 5 thick-tufted pyramidal tract neurons show optimal h-current (HCN channel) responsiveness to 3-6 Hz sinusoidal injection, with the attractor at the upper edge of this resonance range (see [[cortical-layer-oscillations]], [[neurochemistry-of-oscillations]]). Somatostatin-positive (SST+) Martinotti interneurons fire at ~6-8 Hz, contributing to theta generation through dendritic inhibition. The paradox — a functionally central frequency that is empirically depleted in resting dominant-peak analysis — resolves when considering that the enrichment data captures dominant resting-state peaks, not task-evoked transients. The attractor at ~6 Hz is where theta goes during tasks, not where it rests.

**1° Noble at 6.324 Hz (-10% enrichment under EC, 2/5 adult datasets positive).** Near-null in the band-specific enrichment analysis, yet this is where theta generators converge across datasets under overlap-trim extraction. The most striking evidence remains: LEMON eyes-open theta converges to 6.569 Hz and Dortmund eyes-open theta converges to 6.559 Hz — a cross-dataset difference of just 0.009 Hz, arriving from opposite starting frequencies (7.64 Hz and 5.00 Hz under standard extraction). Both land near the Noble1 lattice coordinate (u ≈ 0.633, within 0.015 of the theoretical 1/φ = 0.618). The near-null enrichment is consistent with Noble1 being a transient waypoint: under EO, theta sits here (-4% enrichment), but under EC it migrates upward toward the boundary, draining Noble1 occupancy. The -10% EC value reflects this migration — peaks that were at Noble1 in EO have moved to the boundary and inverse nobles above it. Siebenhühner et al. (2016) found that high theta at 6.6 Hz shows 1:2 harmonic phase-phase coupling with high alpha at 13.2 Hz (see [[cross-frequency-coupling]]). Resting-state theta PFS = 6.54 Hz (Avramescu et al. 2026), dropping to 5.39 Hz during self-face ambiguity processing — a migration from Noble1 toward the attractor/2° Noble under cognitive demand.

Paper 3's "basin interpretation": EC theta converges toward f₀ (7.60 Hz) without reaching it, landing instead near the Noble1 position (6.56 Hz) partway up the potential well. But the enrichment data shows that under stronger EC drive, many generators overshoot Noble1 and continue upward toward the boundary — producing the +281% boundary enrichment and the -10% Noble1 depletion. The Noble1 is a waypoint in the migration, not the final destination. See [[schumann-ignition-events]] for the substrate-ignition model.

## Zone 3: Upper Theta (6.78–7.60 Hz) — The Convergence Corridor

The upper theta zone from the 3° Inverse Noble to f₀ is the most dynamic region — a corridor through which oscillatory generators migrate during state transitions between theta-dominant and alpha-dominant processing.

**3° Inverse Noble at 6.784 Hz (-7% EC, +11% EO).** Near-null in both conditions — a transition zone between the depletion trough and the upper convergence corridor. Unlike the all-band aggregate which showed this as a strong repeller (-25 to -45%), theta specifically shows only mild depletion here. The EC-EO contrast (-22% shift) suggests that EC migration from Noble1 toward the boundary drains this position.

**4° Inverse Noble at 7.085 Hz (+15% EC, +29% EO).** Modestly enriched, with Paper 1's all-peaks analysis showing +47.2% — a theta-specific finding confirmed by the new data. The EO value (+29%) exceeds EC (+15%), suggesting that under EC, theta generators that were at this position migrate further upward toward the boundary. This position represents the lower edge of the convergence corridor — close enough to f₀ for upward [[cross-frequency-coupling|theta-gamma PAC]] while the inverse noble structure resists mode-locking with alpha (see [[critical-frequencies]]).

**6° Inverse Noble at 7.399 Hz — DMT breakthrough zone.** Under DMT, mean peak frequency shifts to 7.36 Hz during breakthrough experiences (see [[psychedelic-states]]) — landing almost exactly at the 6° Inverse Noble. In lattice terms, the brain's dominant rhythm has migrated from a stable noble position to within 0.2 Hz of the boundary, where Fibonacci three-wave resonance enables maximum cross-band energy transfer. This may provide a mechanistic basis for the "everything connected to everything" phenomenology of DMT breakthroughs. Paper 3's best-fit constant for EC theta landing = 7.38 Hz, also in this zone.

**Sigmoid cliff zone (7.40–7.58 Hz).** Paper 2's fine cliff sweep revealed a sigmoid transition in φ's structural advantage spanning ~0.16 Hz, with the zero-crossing at f₀ ≈ 7.55 Hz. The Schumann eigenfrequency prediction (7.49 Hz) falls within this transition region. Below 7.48 Hz, base 2 dominates; above 7.56 Hz, φ achieves rank 1. This zone marks where the φ-lattice "turns on" as an organizational principle (see [[schumann-ignition-events]]).

**Boundary (upper) at 7.600 Hz = f₀ — The absorbing state.** The fundamental frequency of the entire φ-lattice and the theta/alpha fulcrum. Paper 3 demonstrated that this is an absorbing state: 92% of subjects whose theta is at f₀ in eyes-open remain there in eyes-closed, while 61% of Noble1 occupants migrate here under EC. The Dortmund sign-flip definitively identified f₀ as the convergence target: EC theta moves toward f₀ (d = -0.385, p < 10⁻¹⁹) while simultaneously diverging from IAF/2 (d = +0.351, p < 10⁻¹⁶), excluding all IAF-derived models both linear and nonlinear. Migration is bidirectional: subjects with EO theta below f₀ shift upward (+0.38 Hz in LEMON, +0.755 Hz in Dortmund), subjects above f₀ shift downward (-0.22 Hz in LEMON, -0.461 Hz in Dortmund). At stable boundary positions, theta power increases under EC (d = +0.58), while alpha power at its stable positions shows the largest effect size in all three papers (d = +1.00). The lattice is neither fully fixed nor fully dynamic — it is a potential landscape where the f₀ boundary is the deepest well. See [[schumann-ignition-events]] for the relationship to Schumann Resonance (SR monitoring: 7.60 ± 0.20 Hz; agreement within 0.4%).

## Theta's Dynamics Within the Landscape

The 9-dataset enrichment data reveals theta as a **migratory band** — its generators shift dramatically between conditions, producing the U-shaped enrichment profile. The three behavioral modes are now quantified:

**Resting state (EO):** Theta enrichment is more evenly distributed across the upper octave: boundary +175%, 7° Noble +196%, 6° Noble +131%, with moderate inverse noble enrichment (+29 to +102%). The 1° Noble is near-null (-4%). Under EO, theta generators are spread between the boundary zone and the convergence corridor, with some at Noble1 and some already at the boundary. The landscape is U-shaped but less extreme than EC.

**Eyes-closed / increased drive:** EC produces a dramatic upward migration — the upper-boundary enrichment approximately doubles (boundary: +175→+281%, 7° Noble: +196→+349%) while the central trough deepens (attractor: -53→-67%, Noble1: -4→-10%). The EC-EO difference map tells the migration story directly:
- Upper octave gains: boundary +108%, 7° Noble +175%, 6° Noble +125%
- Central octave loses: Noble1 -8%, attractor -15%
- In Dortmund specifically: Noble1 drops from +2.5% (EO) to -45.5% (EC) — a -48% shift, while the boundary gains +163%

This is the absorbing state mechanism quantified: EC increases oscillatory drive, pulling theta generators from the Noble1 waypoint upward toward f₀. The deeper the drive, the more generators complete the migration. Paper 3 showed 92% retention at the boundary, 61% Noble1→boundary migration. The enrichment data is the population-level expression of this per-subject dynamics.

**Task engagement:** Cognitive demand pulls theta generators downward from their resting positions. FM-theta activates at ~5.5 Hz (2° Noble, -75% resting enrichment but functionally active during tasks). Self-ambiguity processing pulls theta PFS to ~5.4 Hz (Avramescu et al. 2026). Global prediction error states fluctuate at 3-6 Hz (Bogdan et al. 2025). The task direction is always downward, deeper into the depleted central trough — theta "overcomes" the depletion to occupy positions where functional computation occurs. The depletion reflects where theta *doesn't rest*, not where it *can't go*.

**Psychedelic disruption:** DMT shifts the dominant frequency to 7.36 Hz (6° Inverse Noble, +86% adult EC enrichment) — nearly at the boundary, maximizing cross-band coupling availability. This places the brain's dominant oscillation in the convergence corridor where enrichment is naturally high. Alpha collapses simultaneously, removing the attractor/Noble1 signal from the alpha octave.

**Pediatric amplification:** The developing brain's theta shows the same U-shape but amplified: boundary +487% (vs adult +281%), 7° Inv Noble +281% (vs adult +136%), with even deeper central depletion (Noble1 -52% vs adult -10%). Children's theta is more polarized — generators concentrate even more strongly at the octave edges, potentially reflecting immature oscillatory dynamics that haven't yet developed the nuanced position-shifting seen in adults.
