The φ-lattice framework generates 25 testable predictions organized by the data required to test them: Tier 1 predictions are testable with existing datasets or standard EEG methods, Tier 2 require new experiments or data types, and Tier 3 are long-range theoretical predictions requiring novel preparations. Two predictions are critical-priority because they address the framework's most important methodological vulnerability (the per-octave extraction dependency) and its most significant measurement limitation (the 45 Hz FOOOF ceiling) — both are testable through re-analysis of existing data with no new recordings. The predictions span enrichment patterns, coupling geometry, developmental trajectories, pharmacological effects, clinical signatures, cross-species generalization, and the fundamental question of whether the lattice is an emergent consequence of molecular kinetics or an independently evolved architectural feature.

## Already Confirmed (Postdictions)

These were predicted by the theoretical framework and subsequently validated empirically — they support the framework but do not constitute prospective tests:

1. **Boundary depletion / noble enrichment** — Predicted by Pletzer (2010), confirmed across 919 subjects in three studies (Lacy 2026, 2026b, 2026c)
2. **Gamma shows strongest adherence** — Predicted from GABA-A precision requirements for temporal binding, confirmed at +144.8% Noble1 enrichment (Paper 1)
3. **f₀ convergence with Schumann Resonance** — Discovered in Paper 1, replicated in Papers 2-3, confirmed by sigmoid cliff at f₀ ≈ 7.55 Hz
4. **φ outperforms rational bases** — Predicted by anti-commensurability theory, confirmed with Cohen's d = 1.22-1.60 irrational advantage (Paper 2)
5. **Exponential, not linear, scaling** — Linear grids produce SS ≈ 0, a categorical negative result (Paper 2)
6. **EC theta convergence on f₀** — Confirmed with Dortmund sign-flip disambiguation (Paper 3)

## Tier 1: Testable With Existing Data or Standard Methods

### From the Band Fingerprints

**P1. Low-beta inverse noble enrichment is universal, not motor-specific.** The original interpretation (Paper 2) was that upper-octave low-beta clustering was a motor paradigm artifact. The 9-dataset data already shows LEMON resting-state at +301%. Prediction: any new resting-state dataset with OT extraction will show 5° Inv Noble enrichment exceeding +100% in low beta. Immediately testable on any public EEG dataset not yet analyzed (see [[low-beta-band-map]]).

**P2. Each band's enrichment shape is invariant across populations with matched age and extraction.** The six shapes (theta U, alpha mountain, low-beta ramp, high-beta bimodal, gamma peak, delta plateau) were derived from 4 adult datasets. Prediction: any adult dataset (N > 100, ages 20-70, OT extraction) will reproduce the same shape — specifically, theta boundary > +200%, alpha attractor > +80%, low-beta 5°InvN > +150%. Failure would indicate dataset-specific rather than species-level organization (see [[phi-lattice-enrichment-data]]).

**P3. Beta must be split at φ² (19.90 Hz) in any study claiming beta effects.** Low beta and high beta have completely different enrichment shapes (ascending ramp vs bimodal). Prediction: re-analyzing any published beta study with a φ² split will reveal sub-band-specific effects masked by pooling. Testable retroactively on any published dataset with beta-band claims (see [[beta-oscillations]]).

### From the f₀ Inversion

**P4. Pathologies affecting one side of the f₀ cliff should inversely affect the other.** The theta-alpha anti-correlation (r = -0.578) predicts coupled effects. In Alzheimer's disease (alpha slows toward f₀), the alpha mountain should flatten while the theta U-curve weakens at the boundary — the cliff erodes. In ADHD (elevated theta/alpha ratio), theta boundary enrichment should increase while alpha attractor enrichment decreases — the cliff becomes asymmetric. Testable with existing clinical EEG datasets analyzed with lattice coordinates (see [[f0-theta-alpha-inversion]], [[clinical-applications]]).

**P5. Sleep onset has a specific lattice temporal sequence.** The cliff model predicts: alpha generators at the attractor/Noble1 lose position first (mountain collapses), then theta generators at the boundary strengthen (well deepens). Prediction: in sleep onset EEG, alpha attractor enrichment will decrease before theta boundary enrichment increases — a temporal ordering, not simultaneous shift. Testable with high-density EEG sleep recordings analyzed in sliding lattice-coordinate windows (see [[sleep-oscillations]]).

### From the Coupling Geometry

**P6. Theta-gamma PAC frequency should track lattice position, not absolute frequency.** If theta-gamma PAC is attractor-to-attractor φ⁴ coupling, the gamma frequency of maximal PAC should equal theta frequency × φ⁴, not a fixed 40 Hz. As theta shifts from ~6.3 Hz (resting Noble1) toward ~7.6 Hz (EC boundary), peak PAC gamma should shift from ~43 Hz to ~52 Hz. If PAC stays locked at 40 Hz regardless of theta frequency, coupling is absolute-frequency-based, not lattice-position-based. Testable with any dataset containing EC/EO conditions and sufficient gamma bandwidth (see [[lattice-coupling-geometry]], [[cross-frequency-coupling]]).

**P7. The 2:1 harmonic map predicts cross-band per-subject enrichment correlations.** The +0.440 shift maps Noble1→6°Noble and Boundary→Attractor across octaves. Prediction: across subjects, enrichment at alpha Noble1 should correlate positively with enrichment at high-beta 6°Noble (harmonically linked positions). Theta boundary enrichment should correlate with alpha attractor enrichment. Testable in the existing 9-dataset enrichment data at the per-subject level (see [[lattice-coupling-geometry]]).

**P8. Predictive coding frequency channels should show φ³ position matching beyond V1-V4.** The Bastos framework shows beta-feedback and gamma-feedforward at 14→60 Hz (both at 3°Noble, ratio ≈ φ³). Prediction: in any cortical hierarchy (auditory, somatosensory, prefrontal), feedback and feedforward frequencies should map to matched lattice positions at approximately φ³ ratios. Testable with laminar recordings across cortical areas (see [[predictive-coding]], [[lattice-coupling-geometry]]).

### From the Developmental Shift

**P9. Gamma enrichment position predicts developmental stage continuously.** The pediatric 2°Noble (+181%) → adult Noble1/Attractor migration tracks GABA-A maturation. Prediction: adolescents (ages 12-18) should show gamma enrichment intermediate between the two positions, shifting monotonically with age. Cross-sectional HBN data stratified by age should show this gradient. More strongly: a discontinuity should appear at the age of GABA-A α2→α1 switch completion (~18-22 years) (see [[development-and-aging]], [[gamma-band-map]]).

**P10. Premature infants should show gamma enrichment at an even lower position.** If developmental migration is driven by GABA-A subunit maturation, very immature brains should show gamma generators at the 3°Noble (36.07 Hz) or 4°Noble (34.54 Hz) — one or two positions below the pediatric 2°Noble. Testable with neonatal EEG datasets (see [[development-and-aging]]).

**P11. Optimizing f₀ for pediatric data should improve φ's rank from 7th.** φ ranks 7th in pediatric HBN under OT extraction with f₀ = 7.83 Hz (adult-optimized). Prediction: optimizing f₀ separately for children will yield a different anchor (possibly lower, reflecting immature thalamocortical loops) and φ's rank will improve. If φ ranks 7th regardless of f₀, the developing brain genuinely uses a different scaling base.

## Tier 2: Require New Experiments or Data Types

### Pharmacological Tests of Molecular Alignment

**P12. GABA-A manipulation should shift gamma's lattice position.** Lorazepam slows GABA-A decay. Prediction: under lorazepam, gamma enrichment should shift from Noble1/Attractor (40-43 Hz) toward the 2°Noble (~39 Hz) — mimicking the pediatric pattern. A 20% increase in decay time (25→30 ms) should shift the peak by approximately one noble position. Conversely, α1-preferring agonists should shift gamma enrichment upward. Requires pharmacological EEG with lattice analysis (see [[oscillator-inventory]], [[neurochemistry-of-oscillations]]).

**P13. M-current blockers should eliminate low-beta's ascending ramp.** If 5°InvNoble enrichment at 19.05 Hz is driven by M-current (KCNQ), then KCNQ blockers (XE991, linopirdine) should selectively flatten the upper-octave enrichment in low beta without affecting other bands. Prediction: under M-current blockade, low-beta's ascending ramp collapses while theta, alpha, and gamma profiles remain unchanged. Requires pharmacological EEG (see [[oscillator-inventory]], [[low-beta-band-map]]).

**P14. T-type Ca²⁺ blockers should reduce the alpha attractor peak.** If alpha attractor enrichment (+113%) depends on thalamocortical loop timing, then T-type blockers (ethosuximide) should reduce attractor enrichment specifically and broaden the alpha peak across a wider lattice range. Requires pharmacological EEG (see [[oscillator-inventory]], [[alpha-band-map]]).

### Cross-Species and f₀ Scaling

**P15. Cross-species scaling should shift f₀ proportionally with brain size.** If f₀ is determined by thalamocortical loop delay (which scales with brain size), rodent f₀ should be higher (~10-12 Hz?) and elephant/whale f₀ lower (~4-5 Hz?). Prediction: lattice analysis of rodent EEG with species-specific f₀ optimization should show the same position hierarchy at a shifted f₀. Requires multi-species EEG with lattice analysis (see [[cross-species-frequency-organization]]).

**P16. The sigmoid cliff should replicate at the same f₀ range in any sufficiently large dataset.** Paper 2 found φ's advantage transitioning across f₀ = 7.40-7.58 Hz. Prediction: this transition should replicate at the same values (±0.1 Hz) in any adult dataset with N > 100 and adequate spectral resolution. The cliff location is an architectural constant. Requires the structural specificity test (Paper 2 methods) applied to new datasets (see [[schumann-ignition-events]]).

### Coupling and Computation

**P17. Task-evoked oscillations should appear at lattice positions depleted in resting data.** The enrichment-coupling paradox shows PAC connects depleted positions. Prediction: working memory tasks should shift theta occupancy from the boundary (resting peak, +281%) toward the attractor (coupling position, -67%). Task-evoked peaks should preferentially appear at positions with negative resting enrichment. Testable with WM-EEG re-analyzed with lattice coordinates (see [[lattice-coupling-geometry]]).

**P18. Resting and coupling positions should map to different cortical layers.** If resting enrichment reflects deep-layer generators (M-current, h-current) while coupling occurs at depleted positions used by superficial-layer generators (PING gamma), then laminar recordings should show different layers active at enriched vs depleted lattice positions. Testable with existing laminar electrode data (see [[cortical-layer-oscillations]], [[lattice-coupling-geometry]]).

**P19. High-beta bimodal peaks should source-localize to different cortical regions.** The ~20 Hz peak (boundary) should originate from sensorimotor cortex (motor beta) and the ~31 Hz peak (7°InvNoble) from prefrontal or parietal cortex (beta-gamma transition). Requires source-localized MEG or high-density EEG (see [[high-beta-band-map]]).

### Extending the Measurement Window

**P20. ~~Extending FOOOF to 80+ Hz should reveal gamma inverse noble structure.~~ CONFIRMED (2026-04-07).** Re-extraction of EEGMMIDB (109 subjects, FREQ_CEIL=75 Hz, no notch filter) and analysis of existing LEMON extraction (202 subjects, FREQ_CEIL=85 Hz, no notch) reveals that the gamma upper octave contains the **strongest enrichment in the band**: 3° Inv Noble +37%, 5° Inv Noble +45%, 6° Inv Noble +49%, 7° Inv Noble +42% (8-dataset mean: 3 adult + 5 HBN pediatric releases; combined N=1,845 excluding CHBMP). The ascending ramp replicates across **8 of 9 datasets** including all 5 HBN pediatric releases (ages 5–21, N=926), establishing the ramp as a developmental invariant. CHBMP (N=250) is the sole anomaly. Cross-release SD at inverse nobles = 1–2%. See [[gamma-band-map]].

**P21. Intracranial EEG should show sharper lattice structure.** Scalp EEG limits spectral resolution and contaminates gamma with muscle artifact. Prediction: intracranial EEG analyzed with the lattice framework should show sharper enrichment peaks, gamma extending above 45 Hz, stronger φ rank without OT extraction, and resolution of the alpha Noble1/IAF confound. Would address Paper 3's recommendation for method-independent validation (see [[gamma-oscillations]], [[aperiodic-dynamics]]).

**P22. IRASA or eBOSC replication should confirm or reject the OT extraction dependency.** Paper 3's most important caveat: φ ranks 1st only under per-octave aperiodic modeling (7th under standard). Prediction: if IRASA-extracted dominant peaks show φ ranking 1st without per-octave fitting, the lattice signal is method-independent. If φ drops to 7th under IRASA, the signal is an artifact of the aperiodic correction step. This is the **single most important test** for the framework's validity. Requires re-analysis only (see [[aperiodic-dynamics]]).

## Tier 3: Long-Range Theoretical Predictions

**P23. Any system with approximately octave-spaced molecular time constants should produce a φ-lattice spontaneously.** If the lattice emerges from competitive dynamics among 2:1-spaced oscillators, then artificial neural networks or neuromorphic chips with ~25, 50, 100, 200 ms time constants should develop φ-organized spectral peaks without any φ-programming. Confirmation would support hypothesis (b) from [[oscillator-inventory]]: the lattice is an emergent mathematical consequence, not an evolutionary tuning (see [[brain-computation]]).

**P24. Organisms with different ion channel kinetics but the same 2:1 ratio should show the same lattice.** Cephalopods have convergently evolved oscillatory activity despite 550 million years of divergence. If octopus GABA-A decay times maintain ~2:1 ratios to equivalent slower conductances, the φ-lattice should appear. If octopus oscillations organize by a different base, the lattice is mammalian-specific (see [[cross-species-frequency-organization]]).

**P25. Disrupting the lattice should impair cognition even though resting alignment doesn't predict it.** The cognitive silence finding (0/100+ tests) means resting alignment doesn't predict performance. But the coupling geometry says computation happens at depleted positions on the lattice scaffold. Prediction: driving oscillations to non-lattice frequencies via tACS at "wrong" positions should impair cognitive performance. The lattice doesn't predict cognition because it's a constant — but removing the constant should reveal its necessity, like removing oxygen doesn't predict running speed but prevents running entirely (see [[unified-phi-lattice-framework]]).
