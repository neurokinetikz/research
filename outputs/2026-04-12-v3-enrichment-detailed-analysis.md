## Key Finding 1: Alpha is the Exact Inverse of Everything Else

The most striking pattern in the entire enrichment landscape: **alpha enrichment is the mathematical inverse of all non-beta-high bands**. At every position, the sign of alpha's enrichment is opposite to theta, beta-low, and gamma.

This creates three octave zones:
- **Lower octave (u=0.0-0.38):** Alpha depleted (-2% to -35%), theta/beta-low/gamma also depleted, beta-high enriched
- **Mid octave (u=0.38-0.62):** Alpha enriched (+29% to +43%), all others depleted or null
- **Upper octave (u=0.62-1.0):** Alpha strongly depleted (-7% to -74%), theta/beta-low/gamma strongly enriched

The alpha-theta anti-correlation is particularly extreme: at boundary_hi, theta is +126% while alpha is -74% -- a 200 pp gap. The position-level correlation between alpha and theta profiles across 13 positions is approximately r ≈ -0.95.

**Interpretation:** Alpha is generated by thalamo-cortical T-type Ca²⁺ channel dynamics with an intrinsic ~100 ms time constant that pins oscillatory peaks to the attractor (~9.67 Hz) and Noble1 (~10.23 Hz). This intrinsic resonance is strong enough to override the upward frequency-gradient tendency that governs all other bands. Where alpha concentrates (mid-octave), it suppresses or displaces other oscillatory modes. Where alpha is absent (upper octave), the gradient-driven accumulation is unopposed.

**Theory comparison:** The original phi-lattice prediction (Pletzer/Kramer) was that Noble1 (φ⁻¹ position) should be universally enriched due to anti-mode-locking. The v3 data show this is true ONLY for alpha. In all other bands, Noble1 is null or weakly enriched (+4% in beta-low, -3% in theta, -13% in beta-high, 0% in gamma). The anti-mode-locking property of Noble1 is exploited by alpha oscillations specifically, not by the brain's oscillatory architecture generally.

## Fibonacci Coupling Analysis (Revised)

### The Fibonacci Property

The defining property of the golden ratio is φ² = φ + 1, which means f(n+2) = f(n+1) + f(n) for any phi-lattice frequencies:

| Equation | Frequencies | Boundary |
|---|---|---|
| f(-1) + f(0) = f(1) | 4.70 + 7.60 = 12.30 | theta/alpha → beta-low |
| f(0) + f(1) = f(2) | 7.60 + 12.30 = 19.90 | alpha/beta-low → beta-high |
| f(1) + f(2) = f(3) | 12.30 + 19.90 = 32.19 | beta-low/beta-high → gamma |
| f(2) + f(3) = f(4) | 19.90 + 32.19 = 52.09 | beta-high/gamma → high-gamma |

The error is exactly 0.0% for exact φ values. This is a mathematical identity, not an empirical approximation. (For rounded display values, 12.30 + 19.90 = 32.20 vs 32.19 -- a 0.01 Hz rounding artifact.)

### Boundary Enrichment: Revised Scorecard

The old analysis claimed boundary enrichment in 4/5 bands as evidence for Fibonacci three-wave resonance. The v3 corrected data tells a different story:

|---|---|---|---|---|
| θ/α | 7.60 | θ **+126%** | α **-35%** | **NO** -- alpha depleted (cliff) |

| βL/βH | 19.90 | βL **+78%** | βH **+86%** | **YES** -- both sides enriched (bridge) |
| βH/γ | 32.19 | βH **-20%** | γ **-4%** | **NO** -- both weakly depleted (weak) |

**Only one boundary (19.90 Hz) shows enrichment from both sides.** The f₀ boundary (7.60 Hz) is a cliff (enriched below, depleted above). The alpha/beta-low boundary (12.30 Hz) is a void (depleted both sides). The beta-high/gamma boundary (32.19 Hz) is weak.

**Revised interpretation:** The 19.90 Hz bridge is consistent with Fibonacci coupling (f(0)+f(1)=f(2), i.e., 7.60+12.30=19.90), but it's the only boundary where this coupling is evident. The other boundaries are dominated by other forces -- intrinsic alpha resonance (f₀ cliff), the ascending ramp gradient (alpha/beta-low void), and the declining 1/f spectral density (beta-high/gamma weak).

The Fibonacci coupling interpretation is **weakened** relative to the old analysis but not eliminated. The ~20 Hz bridge is real and unique. Whether it reflects three-wave resonance or simply the convergence of biophysical time constants (M-current ~50 ms, corticomuscular coherence ~20 Hz, PMBR ~19.4 Hz) at the f₀×φ² frequency is an open question.

### Why Is Alpha Different?

Alpha's inverted pattern (boundary depleted, Noble1 enriched) reflects three converging mechanisms:

1. **Intrinsic resonance dominance:** IAF at ~10 Hz (= attractor/Noble1 zone) is generated by thalamo-cortical T-type Ca²⁺ channel dynamics (~100 ms time constant). This intrinsic oscillator pulls peaks to the center of the octave regardless of frequency-gradient forces.

2. **Anti-mode-locking at Noble1:** φ⁻¹ ≈ 0.618 is the position of maximal resistance to mode-locking (Pletzer/Kramer theory). Alpha oscillations may exploit this property to maintain spectral independence from neighboring bands.

3. **Thalamic pacemaker stability:** The thalamic reticular nucleus generates alpha through burst-mode T-type Ca²⁺ dynamics that are insensitive to cortical frequency gradients. The alpha mountain's shape reflects thalamic constraint, not cortical organization.

The v3 data shows the alpha mountain is broader than previously reported: inv_noble_1 (+39%) and noble_3 (+21%) are now clearly enriched, extending the mountain base from 3 positions (old: attractor, Noble1, inv_noble_1) to 5 positions (noble_3 through inv_noble_3). The mountain is attractor-centered (+43%) with a slight upward bias toward Noble1 (+29%).

- **Consistency:** 12/13 in theta and beta-low, 4/13 in beta-high, 6/13 in gamma
- **Theory match:** PARTIALLY -- the ascending ramp at inverse nobles is consistent with upward energy cascade, but the old "boundary U-shape" interpretation is invalidated

### The Common Thread

Both regimes operate within the phi-lattice coordinate system. The positions that carry enrichment differ by band, but they are ALL phi-lattice positions:
- Alpha: attractor (u=0.500), inv_noble_1 (u=0.382), Noble1 (u=0.618)
- Theta/Beta-low/Gamma: inv_noble_4 (u=0.854) through boundary_hi (u=1.000)
- Beta-high: boundary (u=0.000) through noble_5 (u=0.090)

No peaks cluster at arbitrary positions within the octave. The phi-lattice provides the coordinate system for both regimes -- what differs is which lattice positions are occupied in each band.

The ~20 Hz bridge at f₀×φ² is the point where the two ramps meet -- beta-low ascending from below, beta-high descending from above. This convergence creates the strongest cross-band spectral feature in the entire frequency architecture.

## EC vs EO State Sensitivity

### Per-Band State Effects (LEMON + Dortmund)

| Band | Max |Δ| LEMON | Max |Δ| Dortmund | State pattern |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Theta** | **40 pp** (bnd_hi) | **80 pp** (bnd_hi) | Most state-sensitive. f₀ convergence is EC-specific: boundary_hi drops from +120%/+128% (EC) to +80%/+48% (EO). |
| Alpha | 23 pp (inv_noble_4) | 20 pp (inv_noble_6) | Mountain sharpens under EC: attractor +31→+41 (LEMON), +41→+36 (Dortmund). Noble1 stable: +37→+40 (LEMON). |
| Beta-low | 30 pp (boundary) | 37 pp (boundary) | Ramp attenuates under EO: inv_noble_6 +107→+77 (LEMON). Interior positions stable. |
| Beta-high | 27 pp (boundary) | 42 pp (boundary) | Descending pattern weakens under EO: boundary +23→-4 (LEMON), +52→+10 (Dortmund). |
| Gamma | 6 pp (max) | 14 pp (max) | **State-invariant.** The gamma enrichment profile does not change between EC and EO. |

**Theta f₀ convergence is the strongest state effect in the entire analysis.** Under EC, theta boundary_hi reaches +128% in Dortmund. Under EO, it drops to +48% -- a Δ of 80 pp. This is the "absorbing state" phenomenon: closing the eyes drives theta peaks toward f₀ from below. Opening the eyes disperses them.

**Gamma is state-invariant.** Maximum EC-EO Δ is 6 pp (LEMON) and 14 pp (Dortmund) -- near the noise floor. The gamma ascending ramp is structural, not state-dependent.

### State Sensitivity is Age-Independent

EC→EO delta × age: 0 FDR survivors across 90 tests in both LEMON (N=166, ages 20-77) and Dortmund (N=608, ages 20-70). The AMOUNT of enrichment shift when opening eyes does not change with age.
